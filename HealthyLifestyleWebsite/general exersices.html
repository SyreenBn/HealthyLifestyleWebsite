Regular Plank


1. Start by lying face down on the ground or use an exercise mat .

2. Place your elbows and forearms underneath your chest.

3. Prop yourself up to form a bridge using your toes and forearms

4. Maintain a flat back and do not allow your hips to sag towards the ground.

5. Hold this position, focusing on tightening your abs, until you can no longer maintain a flat bridge.

6. For beginners, start with 10 seconds.

7. Return to start position.

Bicycle


1) While lying on your back on the floor or exercise mat, press your lower back to the floor.

2) Place your hands beside your head, bring your knees to 45-degree angle (halfway between straight up toward the ceiling and level with the floor).

3) Begin a pedal motion by touching opposite elbow to opposite knee, alternating each side.

4) You should feel the tightening and burning in your abs.
5) Start with repetitions of 10-20.

Long-Arm Crunch


1) Lay on your back on the floor or an exercise mat with your arms over your head and knees bent with feet flat on the floor (holding a dumbbell is optional).

2) Curl your shoulders up and towards your knees keeping your arms overhead. Hold 5 seconds.

3) Lower slowly to starting position.

4) Remember to keep head and back in a neutral position. Hyperextension or flexion of either may cause injury.

Paddling/Russian Twist


1) Start by sitting on the floor with hips and knees flexed to approximately 90 degree angles.

2) Grasp a medicine ball or small dumbbell and swing it to the right and left as you keep the hips from rotating with the shoulders.

3) The arms are not perpendicular to the torso, but instead, kept low, near the thighs, as the medicine ball is swung to each side in a paddling motion.

Side Plank


1) Start on your side and press up with your right arm.

2) Form a bridge maintaining a straight line from your hand to your foot.

3) Rest on your elbow to increase the difficulty and hold position for 15-60 seconds.

4) Switch sides and complete the exercise as described.

5) This exercise focuses on the abdominal obliques and transversus abdominus.

Oblique (Elbow to Knee) Crunch


1) Lie flat on your back, bend knees at right angles and point your left knee out while you bring y our left foot up and place it on top of your right knee.

2) Place your fingertips to the side of your head just behind your ears.

3) Curl up just enough to lift both your shoulders off the floor a few inches.

4) Twist so that your right elbow touches your left knee.

5) Hold that position contracting your abs as forcefully as possible as you breathe out.

6) Repeat for the desired number of reps before to switching to the other side.

Leg Raises


1. Lie on your back on an exercise mat. Your legs should be straight out and your toes pointing toward the ceiling. If the mat is large enough, your whole body should be on it. Otherwise, place your backside near the edge of the mat and let your legs hang off the end.

2. Your arms should be relaxed and lying straight by your sides. You can make the exercise slightly easier by placing your hands under your buttocks, but in decreasing the difficulty, you will also decrease your level of benefit. If you are working without a mat and your tailbone starts to hurt, this is a good way to cushion your backside.

3. Lift your legs straight up. Do not arch your back, but keep your back and neck in alignment. You should only use your abdominal muscles for this exercise. Your legs should form a right angle with the rest of your body.

4. Lower your legs. Do not let them rest on the floor, even for a second. Halt your downward motion just before your legs hit the ground. They should remain straight, although it is okay to point your toes.

5. Repeat this exercise as many times as you wish. Generally, trainers recommend that you do 50 to 100 throwdowns at a time. However, if you are just starting, you may prefer to do several sets of 20. You can then increase the number of repetitions as your endurance increases. Remember, it is better to do fewer repetitions at a time and do them correctly than to do more repetitions incorrectly.

Scissor Kicks


1. Lie back onto floor or bench with both knees bent and feet flat. Place hands at sides.

2. Contract abdominal muscles continuously to stabilize trunk (or tuck stomach in by drawing naval in towards spine).

3. Lower back should be in a neutral position.

4. Start position: Straighten both legs so that they are perpendicular to floor.

5. Slowly bring legs out away from each other and then return to side by side.

6. Return to start position and repeat.

Bench Reverse Crunches


1. Start by lying on your back and holding onto the bench with your hands above your head.

2. Keeping your knees bent to 90 degrees left your legs up off the ground and bring your knees up and towards your shoulders.

3. Your range of motion should be far enough so that your hips curl back towards your shoulders as well.

4. Lift your legs and hips with control to avoid jerky motions that create momentum.

5. Return to the starting position and repeat for the desired repetitions.

Flutter Kicks


1. Lie back onto floor or bench with both knees bent and feet flat. Place hands at sides.

2. Contract abdominal muscles continuously to stabilize trunk (or tuck stomach in by drawing naval in towards spine).

3. Lower back should be in a neutral position.

4. Start position: Straighten both legs so that they are perpendicular to floor.

5. Slowly lower one leg to approximately 45-90°.

6. Return to start position and repeat with other leg.

Scorpions


1. Start by getting on your hands and knees in a push-up position.

2. Keeping your abs tight and your trunk parallel bring one knee in towards and across your chest.

3. Return the foot back to the starting position and repeat all repetitions before switching sides.

Twisting Oblique Crunches


1. Lie flat on your back on an exercise mat with your knees bent and your feet flat on the floor.

2. Cross your arms over your chest or your hands behind your back.

3. Crunch your midsection up and across, trying to touch your right elbow to your left knee.

4. Pause, concentrating on contracting your midsection, and twist to try to touch your left elbow to your right knee.

5. Slowly return to the starting position and repeat.

Alternating Single-Leg Plank


1. Get on the floor and prop yourself up with your toes flexed underneath you and your elbows under your shoulders – shoulder blades back and down.

2. Your body should form a straight line.

3. Brace your abs and lift your right leg up about 10 inches.

4. Balance your body weight on your forearms and the stabilizing leg.

5. Hold for up to 60 seconds.

6. Switch legs and repeat on the other side.

2 Point Side Bridge – Single Leg Side Plank


1. Start in a pushup position, then rotate and open one side of your body into the side bridge.

2. At point of lockout, carefully lift the top leg; stabilize; then hold for a 5-30 seconds.

3. Make sure to keep your neck in a neutral position so it isn’t strained.

4. Alternate back and forth on both sides holding each abduction if possible.

Crunches


1. Start position: Lie back on the floor or bench with knees bent and hands behind head.

2. Keep elbows back and out of sight.

3. Head should be in a neutral position with a space between chin and chest.

4. Leading with the chin and chest towards the ceiling, contract the abdominal and raise shoulders off floor or bench.

5. Return to start position.

6. Remember to keep head and back in a neutral position. Hyperextension or flexion of either may cause injury.

Lying Side Crunches


1) Lie with back on floor or bench with knees bent.

2) Start position: Let your knees fall to the right so that your hips are somewhat rotated.

3) Leading with the chin and left shoulder, contract abdominal muscles and raise left shoulder off floor or bench towards left knee.

4) Return to start position. Repeat with other side.

5) Remember to keep head and neck in neutral position throughout movement. Do not twist excessively – elbow does not need to touch knee.

Alternating Superman Plank


1. Start the movement in a plank position.

2. While holding that position, raise your right arm and left leg off of the ground.

3. Return to the starting position and repeat with the other arm and leg.

4. Hold each lift for 1-2 seconds and repeat for the desired number or repetitions.

Cobra on Floor


1. Start by lying on your stomach with your arms at your sides.

2. Slowly raise your shoulders up off the ground by contracting your low back.

3. Return to the starting position and repeat.

Alternating Toe Touch


1. Lie back onto floor or bench with feet up into the air.

2. Head should be in a neutral position with a space between chin and chest.

3. Leading with the chin and chest towards the ceiling, contract the abdominal and raise shoulders off floor or bench.

4. Extend one arm and reach for the opposite foot.

5. Return to start position and repeat with other hand.

6. Remember to keep head and back in a neutral position; hyperextension or flexion may cause injury.

7. To increase resistance, hold medicine ball in hands; to decrease resistance, position hand closer towards body.

Side Plank Crunches


1. Place forearm right under shoulder and start in side bridge position with “stacked” feet, top arm up, and fingertips on ear.

2. While keeping hips up in bridge position, twist top elbow down and touch floor as you “crunch” core muscles.

3. Return to full starting position then repeat all repetitions on one side before changing positions.

Straight Leg Hip Thrusts


1. Lie on your back with your legs bent 90 degrees at the hip.

2. Slowly lift your hips off the floor and towards the ceiling.

3. Lower your hips to the floor and repeat for the prescribed number of repetitions.

Mountain Climber on Floor or Exercise Ball


1. Start in the top of the push-up position on the floor and brace your abs (execute this exercise by placing your hands on top of an exercise ball for the more advanced).

2. Keep your abs braced as you pick one foot up off the floor, and slowly bring your knee up to your chest.

3. Do not let your hips sag or rotate.

4. Keep your abs braced and slowly return your leg to the start position.

5. Alternate sides until you complete all of the required repetitions.

Parallel Bar Abdominal Knee Raises


1. Step up onto apparatus and place forearms into pads.

2. Grasp handles and stabilize shoulders by depressing shoulders down.

3. Start position: bring knees up with hip flexed at approximately 90°.

4. Keeping back firmly pressed against back support, slowly lower legs.

5. Return to start position.

6. Remember to keep head and back in a neutral position. Keep stomach tight (naval drawn in towards spine) throughout entire movement.

3. Lower back should be in a neutral position.

4. Start position: Straighten both legs so that they are perpendicular to floor.
